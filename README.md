# Sample-Superstore-Analysis-Report


## Reporting Period: Up to May 24, 2015  
Tools used: Excel, SQL, Power BI  
Report Type: Sales Performance Dashboard

## Overview
This dashboard provides a comprehensive analysis of the Superstore’s sales performance, profitability, cost of goods sold (COGS), and product distribution across states, cities, and regions.

## Key Highlights
- Total Sales: 11,488,062  
- Total COGS:11,201,665  
- Total Profit: 286,397  
- Number of Sales Transactions: 2,297,201

## Breakdown by Categories
- Top States by Sales:
  - California leads with2.3M+
  - New York and Texas also show high sales performance

- Profit by Sub-Category:
  - Most profitable: Phones, Chairs, Copiers
  - Losses observed in categories like Tables and Bookcases

- COGS Trend Over Time:
  - Fluctuating trends from 2011 to 2017
  - Spikes in COGS at multiple intervals suggest seasonal demand or supply chain impacts

## Regional and City Performance

- By Region:
  - *West* contributes the most to sales (725.46K), followed by *East* and *Central*
  
- By City (Quantity Sold):
  - New York City, Los Angeles, and Philadelphia lead in order quantities
  - Smaller volumes recorded in cities like San Diego and Springfield

## Insights & Recommendations
- Profit Margins are relatively low compared to total sales, suggesting high operational costs.
- Focus on low-performing sub-categories (e.g., Tables) to assess profitability.
- Regional strategies may be optimized further — West is leading, while South shows lower figures.
